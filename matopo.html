About Matopo National Park

Nestled in the heart of Zimbabwe, Matopo National Park is renowned for its unique granite rock formations, which have been shaped by erosion over millions of years, this monument holds a rich cultural heritage and is home to a diverse range of wildlife.

Discover the diverse flora and fauna that call this park home, including the endangered black rhino and a variety of bird species. Take a guided tour to learn about the park's conservation efforts and the importance of preserving its natural beauty.

The Historical Significance

Matopo National Park holds great historical and cultural significance. It is home to ancient rock art sites, showcasing the rich heritage of the indigenous people who once inhabited this area.

Learn about the spiritual beliefs and rituals associated with these rock art sites, and gain a deeper understanding of the cultural heritage of the region.

Must-See Attractions

Experience the beauty of the park through its stunning attractions. From the iconic balancing rocks to the ancient cave paintings, each attraction tells a unique story.
